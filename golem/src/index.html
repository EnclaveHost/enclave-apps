machines animated in your browser — OS images unsealed only inside the enclave
Your machine images live encrypted in your own S3 bucket; the wallet that signs is the only key. Unlock decrypts them onto the enclave's ramdisk, and this page boots them with QEMU compiled to WebAssembly — the CPU runs in your browser tab, fed by the attested enclave. Save disk streams a guest-disk snapshot back into the volume; Sync re-encrypts it to your bucket.

How this works. The App Config names where each volume's ciphertext lives (S3 endpoint, bucket, path) — never a key. Unlock with wallet signs a fixed message naming the volume; the crypt key is derived from that signature locally, relayed only to this app over the deployment's in-enclave-terminated TLS, and passed over loopback to the in-enclave manager, which pulls and decrypts your machine images onto the CVM's encrypted ramdisk. This page then loads QEMU-wasm and the guest image from the enclave — served cross-origin-isolated (COOP/COEP), which SharedArrayBuffer threading requires and static S3 hosting can't provide. The emulated CPU runs in this tab; the enclave is the sealed image vault. Save disk writes a snapshot into the volume; Sync re-encrypts to your bucket; Lock wipes the plaintext. Verify the code holding your images with the deployment's remote attestation at enclave.host.
